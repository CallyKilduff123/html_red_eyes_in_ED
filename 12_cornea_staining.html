Question: Does the cornea stain with fluorescein?



Corneal staining

Apply fluorescein drop to cornea. Shine blue light. Any areas of stain will fluoresce. This suggests breach in the corneal epithelial layer.

No stain

There do not appear to be any areas of staining on the cornea (ensure the lid is lifted to fully expose the whole cornea).

Most emergency departments have fluorescein drops and there is a blue light function on the slit-lamp: Demonstration video. Some smartphone adjuncts also have blue-light capability.
