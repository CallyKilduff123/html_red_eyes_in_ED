ENDOPHTHALMITIS


Often within 6 weeks of intraocular surgery or injection, or post-trauma to the eye, the patient presents with pain +/- photophobia. Redness. They will likely experience reduced vision in the affected eye. Eyelid swelling is often present. They may also have an RAPD and/or a visible hypopyon.

This is a time-critical condition. The ophthalmology team will need to perform a procedure to sample the fluid from the eye and deliver antibiotics via injection or they will take the patient to theatre immediately, depending on the visual acuity.

If endophthalmitis is suspected, clinicians are advised to refer immediately to ophthalmology, regardless of time of day or night.

Clinicians are always advised to have a very low threshold to suspect endophthalmitis in post-operative patients presenting with a red eye within 4-6 weeks.
