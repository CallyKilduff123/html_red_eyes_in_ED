FACIAL NERVE (VII) PALSY


Weakness of 1 side of face / facial asymmetry (NB can be bilateral). Lagophthalmos +/- lower lid ectropion (turning out). NB: Classic teaching: if the forehead is not affected (i.e. the patient is able to raise fully the eyebrow on the affected side) then the facial palsy is likely to be an upper motor neuron (UMN) lesion. Paralysis which includes the forehead is likely a lower motor neuron (LMN) lesion.

Ocular surface protection is paramount, because the cornea is exposed: Topical lubricants hourly with ointment at night. Tape the eye shut at night.

If lagophthalmos with red eye and corneal staining with fluorescein, the ophthalmology team would want to see this patient within 24h (i.e. in eye casualty). All other cases are often referred routinely.

These patients are often under joint care with medics to establish the cause of new CN VII palsies and exclude intracranial pathology. Recurrent CN VII palsies should have neuroimaging (exclude neoplastic/inflammatory causes)
