ACUTE ANGLE CLOSURE


Systemic features include: pain (periocular, headache, abdominal), blurred vision, haloes, nausea and vomiting. Ocular features include: eye pain not improving with topical anaesthetic drops, the globe is hard on palpation (it can be compared to the other eye or to the examiner's eye), acute onset unilateral red eye, cloudy cornea (iris and pupil not clear to see) +/- fixed mid-dilated oval pupil.

Systemic: 500mg IV or PO Acetazolamide. Topical: Any or all of the following: Beta-blocker - e.g. Timolol 0.5%, Carbonic anhydrase inhibitor e.g. Dorzolamide, or mixed e.g. Cosopt (Dorzolamide & Timolol). Sympathomimetic e.g. Apraclonidine 1% stat. Steroid e.g. Prednisolone 1% stat.

The patient will need immediate referral to the ophthalmology team (night or day) for IOP-lowering treatment, including laser peripheral iridotomy.

This is a time-critical eye emergency because an acute rise in IOP causes irreversible optic nerve damage. It is also advisable to Warn patients of the side effects of Acetazolamide and use with caution/ seek advice in renal impairment.
