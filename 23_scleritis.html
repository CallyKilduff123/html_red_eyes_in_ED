SCLERITIS


Pain (severe, constant, deep / retrobulbar, boring). Pain may wake patient from sleep. Pain on eye movement - may radiate to jaw and neck. The eye may be tender to touch/ palpate +/- Bluish hue to sclera. Redness may be diffuse or nodular. Redness does not blanche with topical vasoconstrictor e.g. 2.5% -10% phenylepherine). 50% have systemic disease (connective tissue disorder). Bilateral in 50%.

PO NSAIDs with PPI cover. Consider baseline bloods (FBC, CRP, LFTs,U+Es, ESR and Vasculitic screen) +/ - consider investigations for TB (CXR) , Sarcoid (ACE) & Syphillis serology.

These patients are referred to ophthalmology urgently (within 12h). They may also be referred to rheumatology if symptoms are severe or if there is suspected systemic involvement.

Posterior scleritis may present with a white eye. If reduced vision and pain, particularly in a patient with rheumatoid arthritis or vasculitis discuss with rheumatology and ophthalmology
